## Your First Facial: What No One Tells You

Booking your first professional facial is exciting — and it can also feel a little mysterious. What should you do beforehand? What will actually happen during the appointment? What about afterward? 

The good news: preparing for a facial is simple. And knowing what to expect makes the experience significantly better — both in terms of comfort and results.

## Before Your Appointment

### Stop Retinoids and Strong Exfoliants (3–5 Days Before)
If you use retinol, tretinoin, or any strong chemical exfoliants (AHAs, BHAs, or prescription acids), pause them 3–5 days before your facial. Using these actives too close to your appointment can make your skin more sensitive and prone to irritation during treatment.

### Skip Waxing or Threading (at Least 5 Days Before)
Any hair removal around the face should be done at least 5 days prior, or waited until after your facial. Freshly waxed or threaded skin is more vulnerable to irritation.

### Come with a Clean-ish Face
You don't need to arrive makeup-free — your esthetician will cleanse your skin as part of the treatment. But if you're wearing heavy makeup or products, it helps to remove the bulk of it beforehand so your appointment time is spent treating rather than cleansing.

### Be Ready to Share Your Skin History
Before your facial begins, you'll have a brief consultation. Come prepared to discuss: your current skin care routine, any products or ingredients you've reacted to in the past, current medications, and what concerns you'd most like to address. The more your esthetician knows, the more personalized your treatment can be.

### Don't Schedule Right Before a Big Event
For your first facial, leave a few days buffer before any major occasion. While most facials leave skin looking great immediately, some treatments (especially those involving deeper extractions) can cause temporary redness or slight purging. By day two or three, your skin will look its best.

## During Your Facial

### You're in Control
If anything feels uncomfortable — pressure, temperature, a product stinging — say so immediately. A great esthetician adjusts in real time and wants your feedback. This isn't a place to tough it out.

### The Extraction Phase
If your facial includes extractions (clearing blackheads and congestion), this is typically the least comfortable part. It can feel like brief pinching. It's normal, and it's also extremely beneficial — professional extractions done correctly are far safer than DIY attempts at home.

### Enjoy the Rest
Between active treatment steps, you'll experience massage, masking, and relaxation. This isn't wasted time — the massage work stimulates circulation and lymphatic drainage, contributing directly to your results. Let yourself relax into it.

## After Your Facial

### Keep It Simple for 24–48 Hours
For the first day or two after your facial, keep your skin care routine minimal: a gentle cleanser, a good moisturizer, and broad-spectrum SPF. Skip your retinoids, strong acids, and any new products. Your skin is freshly treated and more receptive — also more vulnerable.

### Wear Sunscreen (Seriously)
Your freshly exfoliated skin is more susceptible to UV damage. Wear SPF daily — this is non-negotiable after any professional facial. If you weren't in the sunscreen habit before, now's the time to start.

### Don't Pick or Squeeze
After extractions, some pores may look slightly more noticeable temporarily, or you might see a small breakout emerge as congestion works its way out. Do not pick at your skin. This is part of the process, and it will clear.

### Follow Your Esthetician's Home-Care Recommendations
One of the most valuable parts of a professional facial is the guidance you receive afterward. Your esthetician knows your skin — their product and routine recommendations are tailored to maximize your results between sessions.

### Consider Booking Your Next Session
The benefits of professional skin care compound over time. Most clients see their best results after a series of treatments. Ask about scheduling your next appointment before you leave — many treatment programs work best with consistent 3–4 week intervals.
