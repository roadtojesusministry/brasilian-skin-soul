## The Problem with Most Skin Care Routines

Most people build their skin care routine backwards. They start with the products — the latest serum trending on social media, the moisturizer with the best reviews, the eye cream their favorite influencer swears by — and hope that layering enough things will eventually produce results.

The reality is simpler, and slightly more demanding: an effective routine starts with understanding your skin, then building strategically around that understanding.

## Step One: Know Your Skin Type (Really Know It)

There's a significant difference between skin that's oily because it's dehydrated (and producing excess oil to compensate) and skin that's genuinely oilier by nature. Dry and dehydrated are different conditions. Sensitive skin and reactive skin aren't the same thing.

Getting clarity on your actual skin type and concerns — ideally with a professional assessment — is the most valuable first investment you can make. It prevents you from spending months using the wrong products and wondering why nothing is working.

## The Core Four: What Every Routine Needs

Regardless of your skin type or concerns, every effective routine contains four foundational elements:

### 1. Cleanser
Cleanse morning and evening with a formulation appropriate for your skin type. Over-cleansing strips your barrier; under-cleansing allows buildup to accumulate. A gentle, pH-balanced cleanser is the starting point for almost everyone.

### 2. Treatment
This is where you address your specific concerns — a vitamin C serum for brightening, a retinol for cell turnover, a niacinamide for pore minimization and oil balance, a hydrating serum for dehydrated skin. Choose one or two active treatments and use them consistently rather than rotating through everything.

### 3. Moisturizer
Every skin type needs moisture — even oily skin. A good moisturizer seals in your treatment, maintains your barrier function, and keeps skin resilient. Match the weight of your moisturizer to your skin type: lighter gels for oily skin, richer creams for dry skin.

### 4. Sunscreen (Every Morning, No Exceptions)
This one is non-negotiable. UV damage is the leading cause of visible aging, hyperpigmentation, and skin damage. Broad-spectrum SPF 30 or higher, every morning, even on overcast days. If you do nothing else on this list, do this.

## The Most Common Routine Mistakes

**Using too many actives at once.** Retinol + vitamin C + AHA + BHA in one routine is a recipe for irritation, not results. Build slowly, introduce one new active at a time, and let your skin adapt.

**Inconsistency.** Skin care works through consistency, not intensity. A simple routine used every day beats an elaborate one used sporadically.

**Skipping moisturizer if you're oily.** This leads to more oil production, not less. Your skin produces excess oil when it's lacking moisture — give it what it needs.

**Expecting overnight results.** Most actives take 6–12 weeks of consistent use to show visible improvement. Give your routine time before declaring it doesn't work.

**Ignoring the neck and chest.** Your skin care doesn't end at your jawline.

## Building Your Routine: A Starting Point

If you're starting from scratch or resetting after a period of chaos:

1. **Cleanse** (AM + PM) with a gentle, appropriate cleanser
2. **Apply one treatment serum** suited to your primary concern
3. **Moisturize** with something appropriate for your skin type
4. **SPF** every morning (this cannot be skipped)
5. **Book a professional facial** to reset your skin and get a personalized recommendation

That's it. Start simple, stay consistent, and let your esthetician guide the rest. Skin care doesn't have to be complicated — it just has to be right for your skin.
